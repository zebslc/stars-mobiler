# Racial Traits System

## Overview

The Racial Traits system is the foundation of Stars!' replayability and strategic depth. Each species must select **one Primary Racial Trait (PRT)** and can have multiple **Lesser Racial Traits (LRTs)** that fundamentally alter their gameplay experience. These traits determine which ship components are available, providing exclusive abilities or imposing restrictions that create distinct playstyles for each species.

The trait system is fully data-driven and integrated into the component filtering pipeline. Players will never see components they cannot use, ensuring a clean and intuitive interface.

## Primary Racial Traits (PRTs)

Each species has exactly **ONE** Primary Racial Trait that defines their core identity and philosophy. This choice is permanent and has the most significant impact on gameplay.

### Jack of All Trades (JOAT)
**Theme:** Balanced generalists with faster, more flexible research progression

**Benefits:**
- Faster research across all tech fields
- Reduced research costs
- No component restrictions

**Drawbacks:**
- No exclusive components
- No special abilities

**Strategy:** Well-rounded approach suitable for beginners. Flexible tech tree allows adaptation to any situation. No unique components means relying on standard tools and superior tech levels.

**Used By:** Terrans

---

### Super Stealth (SS)
**Theme:** Advanced cloaking technology and espionage specialists

**Benefits:**
- Reduced ship visibility to enemy scanners
- Exclusive access to cloaking devices (2 components)
- Exclusive stealth shields and armor (2 components)
- Cargo stealing scanners (3 components)
- Advanced scanners with stealth bonuses (6 components)
- **Total: 13 exclusive components**

**Drawbacks:**
- No inherent drawbacks (balanced by expensive components)

**Strategy:** Scout and raider playstyle. Cloaked ships for reconnaissance, cargo theft from enemy fleets, stealth bonuses make detection difficult. Excellent for gathering intelligence and surgical strikes.

**Used By:** Pyreans

---

### Inner Strength (IS)
**Theme:** Defensive specialists immune to biological warfare

**Benefits:**
- Immunity to biological weapons
- Exclusive Dual Shield (shield + armor hybrid, 1 component)
- Exclusive Jammers (torpedo deflection, 2 components)
- Exclusive Cloak Detection (counter-stealth, 1 component)
- **Total: 6 exclusive components**

**Restrictions:**
- **Cannot use Smart Bombs** (5 advanced bombing components blocked)
- Biological weapons don't work on silicon-based life

**Drawbacks:**
- Limited high-tech bombing capabilities
- Reduced offensive options

**Strategy:** Defensive warfare and mining focus. Excellent at protecting fleets with jammers and hybrid shields. Strong miners but weak at planetary bombardment. Trade offense for defense.

**Used By:** Crystallids

---

### Interstellar Traveler (IT)
**Theme:** Natural space travelers with excellent fuel efficiency

**Benefits:**
- Inherent fuel efficiency bonus
- Faster fleet movement
- Exclusive Fuel Cell (generates fuel + storage, 1 component)

**Drawbacks:**
- No inherent drawbacks

**Strategy:** Expansion-focused playstyle. Excellent range and endurance for deep-space operations. Can reach distant stars faster and maintain larger fleets in the field. Ideal for aggressive expansion and hit-and-run tactics.

**Used By:** Voidborn

---

### Hyper Expansion (HE)
**Theme:** Rapid colonization specialists with growth bonuses

**Benefits:**
- Double population growth rate
- Faster planet development
- Exclusive Settler's Delight engine (mini-colonizer only, 1 component)
- Exclusive Energy Capacitor (20% beam damage bonus, 1 component)

**Drawbacks:**
- 50% research penalty (very slow tech progression)
- Maximum population per planet halved

**Strategy:** Early game expansion rush. Colonize aggressively, overwhelm with numbers. Accept slower tech progression in exchange for territorial dominance.

**Used By:** *Currently unused - available for custom species*

---

### War Monger (WM)
**Theme:** Military specialists with combat bonuses

**Benefits:**
- Access to advanced military hulls earlier
- Inherent accuracy bonus in combat
- Reduced ship weapon costs

**Drawbacks:**
- Higher ship armor/defense costs

**Strategy:** Aggressive military expansion. Superior combat effectiveness and earlier access to dreadnoughts. Dominate through military might.

**Used By:** *Currently unused - available for custom species*

**Note:** No components currently require/restrict this trait.

---

### Claim Adjuster (CA)
**Theme:** Salvage and insurance specialists

**Benefits:**
- Exclusive Retro Bomb (unterraforms planets, 1 component)
- Higher mineral recovery from destroyed ships
- Bonus resources from abandoned planets

**Drawbacks:**
- Cannot bomb your own planets for insurance purposes

**Strategy:** Reclamation warfare. Unterraform enemy planets to make them less habitable, forcing abandonment. Profit from salvage and ruins.

**Used By:** *Currently unused - available for custom species*

---

### Space Demolition (SD)
**Theme:** Mining and destruction specialists

**Benefits:**
- Exclusive Battle Computer (slows enemy movement, 1 component)
- Enhanced mining capabilities
- Bonus from minefield sweeping

**Drawbacks:**
- Limited research in non-weapon fields

**Strategy:** Industrial warfare. Excellent miners and demolition experts. Strong in asteroid mining and minefield tactics.

**Used By:** *Currently unused - available for custom species*

---

### Packet Physics (PP)
**Theme:** Mass driver warfare specialists

**Benefits:**
- Mass drivers become weapons (no decay over distance)
- Can shoot mineral packets at enemy planets for damage
- Enhanced logistics network

**Drawbacks:**
- Requires significant infrastructure investment

**Strategy:** Long-range artillery warfare. Build mass driver networks and bombard enemies from across the galaxy. Requires strong economy and defensive positions.

**Used By:** *Currently unused - available for custom species*

**Note:** No components currently require/restrict this trait.

---

### Alternate Reality (AR)
**Theme:** Energy-based lifeforms with inverted economy

**Benefits:**
- Population doesn't consume standard resources
- Unique economic model
- Different colonization priorities

**Drawbacks:**
- Consumes energy instead of resources
- Inverted resource management

**Strategy:** Fundamentally different gameplay. Energy management becomes critical. Alien ecology leads to unique expansion patterns.

**Used By:** *Currently unused - available for custom species*

**Note:** No components currently require/restrict this trait.

## Lesser Racial Traits (LRTs)

Species can have 2-4 Lesser Racial Traits, mixing advantages and disadvantages to fine-tune their capabilities. LRTs modify the PRT's core identity with specific bonuses and penalties.

### Advantages

#### Improved Fuel Efficiency (IFE)
- Fuel usage reduced by 15%
- **Grants access to:** Fuel Mizer engine (1 component)
- Excellent synergy with Interstellar Traveler
- **Used by:** Pyreans, Voidborn

#### Generalized Research (GR)
- Flexible research progression
- Can research multiple fields simultaneously at reduced penalty
- Complements JOAT and Super Stealth
- **Used by:** Terrans, Pyreans

#### Improved Starbases (ISB)
- Starbase hull costs reduced
- Enhanced starbase capabilities
- Better orbital defense
- **Used by:** Terrans

#### Advanced Remote Mining (ARM)
- Fleets can mine longer without returning
- Reduced remote mining decay
- Enhanced asteroid mining
- **Used by:** Crystallids

#### Ultimate Recycling (UR)
- 90% mineral recovery from scrapped ships (vs standard 33%)
- Excellent resource efficiency
- Complements mining-focused species
- **Used by:** Crystallids

#### Total Terraforming (TT)
- Can terraform beyond normal limits
- Unlock extreme habitat modifications
- Create green worlds from hostile planets
- **Used by:** Voidborn

#### Mineral Alchemy (MA)
- Convert minerals between types (e.g., Ironium â†’ Germanium)
- Flexible resource management
- Excellent for space-based economy
- **Used by:** Voidborn

#### Bleeding Edge Technology (BET)
- Access to advanced tech earlier
- Reduced tech level requirements
- Faster progression to end-game components
- **Used by:** Pyreans

**Drawback:** Advanced tech costs more resources to build

#### Regenerating Shields (RS)
- Shields regenerate between combat rounds
- Enhanced defensive capabilities
- Synergy with shield-heavy designs
- **Used by:** *Currently unused*

#### Cheap Engines (CE)
- Engine components cost fewer resources
- Economic advantage for large fleets
- Good for expansion-focused species
- **Used by:** Terrans

### Disadvantages

Disadvantages reduce the "cost" of a species design, allowing more advantage traits to be selected in a point-buy system (not yet implemented).

#### No Ramscoop Engines (NRSE)
- Blocks 4 ramscoop engine types
- **Grants access to:** Galaxy Scoop (superior alternative)
- Net gain: Better technology at cost of flexibility
- **Used by:** Voidborn

#### Only Basic Remote Mining (OBRM)
- Limited to basic remote mining technology
- Cannot research advanced mining tech
- Balances Advanced Remote Mining advantage
- **Used by:** Crystallids

#### No Advanced Sensors (NAS)
- Blocks 3 advanced scanner types (Ferret, Dolphin, Gazelle)
- Limited reconnaissance capabilities
- Forces reliance on standard scanners
- **Used by:** Crystallids

#### Low Starting Population (LSP)
- Start with fewer colonists
- Slower early expansion
- Takes longer to reach critical mass
- **Used by:** Terrans, Pyreans

**Trade-off:** Balances strong advantages with slower start

## Species Trait Assignments

### Terrans - Balanced Generalists
**Primary:** Jack of All Trades
**Lesser:** Generalized Research, Improved Starbases, Cheap Engines, Low Starting Population

**Lore:** Humans are adaptable generalists who excel at nothing but adapt to everything. Their balanced approach to technology and versatile research capabilities make them ideal for players new to Stars!.

**Components Available:**
- All standard components
- No exclusive components
- No restrictions

**Playstyle:**
- Beginner-friendly balanced approach
- Faster research progression compensates for no special components
- Superior tech levels provide advantage without special abilities
- Defensive focus with Improved Starbases
- Economic efficiency with Cheap Engines
- Jack of all trades, master of none

**Weaknesses:**
- No unique capabilities
- Slow early expansion (Low Starting Population)
- Must rely on tech superiority rather than special abilities

---

### Crystallids - Mining & Defense Specialists
**Primary:** Inner Strength
**Lesser:** Advanced Remote Mining, Ultimate Recycling, Only Basic Remote Mining, No Advanced Sensors

**Lore:** Silicon-based lifeforms adapted to extreme mining environments. Their crystalline structure provides excellent defensive capabilities but limits biological warfare applications. Masters of resource extraction and defensive warfare.

**Components Gained (6):**
1. Dual Shield (Croby Sharmor) - 60 shields + 65 armor hybrid
2. Jammer 10 - 10% torpedo deflection chance
3. Jammer 50 - 50% torpedo deflection chance
4. Cloak Detection System - Reduces enemy cloaking effectiveness by 5%
5-6. Additional Inner Strength components

**Components Lost (8):**
1. Smart Bomb (biological)
2. Neutron Bomb (biological)
3. Enriched Neutron Bomb (biological)
4. Peerless Bomb (biological)
5. Annihilator (biological)
6. Ferret Scanner (advanced sensor)
7. Dolphin Scanner (advanced sensor)
8. Gazelle Scanner (advanced sensor)

**Playstyle:**
- Defensive powerhouse with jammers deflecting torpedoes
- Excellent mining with ARM and Ultimate Recycling
- Strong resource economy (90% scrap recovery)
- Limited high-tech bombing (no smart bombs)
- Reduced scanning capabilities (no advanced sensors)
- Trade offense for defense

**Weaknesses:**
- Weak at planetary bombardment (no biological weapons)
- Limited reconnaissance (No Advanced Sensors)
- Must rely on kinetic/conventional weapons
- Defensive rather than offensive military

---

### Pyreans - Research & Stealth Specialists
**Primary:** Super Stealth
**Lesser:** Generalized Research, Improved Fuel Efficiency, Bleeding Edge Technology, Low Starting Population

**Lore:** Adapted to extreme heat environments, Pyreans developed advanced stealth technology to survive. Their focus on cutting-edge research and cloaking capabilities makes them excellent scouts, spies, and raiders.

**Components Gained (14):**
1. Transport Cloaker - 75% cloak for unarmed ships
2. Cloak - 85% cloak for any ship
3. Stealth Shield - 75 shield + 35% stealth bonus
4. Shadow Armor - 200 armor + 25% stealth bonus
5. Cargo Tractor Scanner (80 LY) - Can steal enemy cargo
6. Shadow Scanner (160 LY) - Scanning with 20% stealth bonus
7. Robber Baron Scanner (220 LY) - Advanced cargo stealing
8-13. Six additional stealth scanners with cloaking bonuses
14. Fuel Mizer engine (from Improved Fuel Efficiency)

**Components Lost:** None

**Playstyle:**
- Stealth scouts and raiders
- Cloaked ships for reconnaissance
- Cargo theft from enemy fleets
- Advanced scanning with stealth bonuses
- Research superiority (Generalized Research + Bleeding Edge Tech)
- Excellent fuel efficiency for long-range ops
- Slow expansion (Low Starting Population)

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive technology (Bleeding Edge Technology cost penalty)
- Slow population growth limits early expansion
- Stealth components are mass/cost intensive
- Requires tech superiority to leverage advantages

**Balance Note:** 14 exclusive components balanced by expensive tech costs and slow expansion

---

### Voidborn - Space Travel & Expansion Specialists
**Primary:** Interstellar Traveler
**Lesser:** Improved Fuel Efficiency, Total Terraforming, No Ramscoop Engines, Mineral Alchemy

**Lore:** Adapted to harsh, low-oxygen environments, Voidborn are natural space travelers. Their excellent fuel efficiency and terraforming mastery enables rapid expansion across hostile worlds.

**Components Gained (3):**
1. Fuel Cell - 200mg fuel storage + generates 50mg/year
2. Fuel Mizer engine (from Improved Fuel Efficiency)
3. Galaxy Scoop - Superior ramscoop technology (better than standard ramscoops)

**Components Lost (4):**
1. Fuel Mizer 1 (standard ramscoop)
2. Fuel Mizer 2 (standard ramscoop)
3. Fuel Mizer 3 (standard ramscoop)
4. Fuel Mizer 4 (standard ramscoop)

**Net Trade:** Lose 4 standard ramscoops but gain superior Galaxy Scoop (tech upgrade)

**Playstyle:**
- Excellent range and endurance for deep-space operations
- Stack fuel bonuses: IT + IFE + Fuel Mizer = exceptional efficiency
- Fast expansion with superior fuel economy
- Terraform hostile worlds (Total Terraforming)
- Flexible resource management (Mineral Alchemy)
- Convert minerals between types as needed
- Long-range fleets can operate far from home

**Weaknesses:**
- No exclusive combat capabilities
- Standard component access (no special weapons/defense)
- Must leverage mobility and logistics for advantage

**Strengths:**
- Best expansion capabilities of any species
- Can colonize and develop hostile planets faster
- Superior fleet logistics enable aggressive positioning
- Economic flexibility with mineral conversion

## Gameplay Impact Analysis

### Component Access Summary

| Species | Exclusive Components | Blocked Components | Net Change | Special Abilities |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|------------|-------------------|
| Terrans | 0 | 0 | 0 | Faster research |
| Crystallids | 6 | 8 | -2 | Defensive warfare, mining bonuses |
| Pyreans | 14 | 0 | +14 | Stealth, espionage, cargo theft |
| Voidborn | 3 | 4* | +1* | Superior fuel economy, expansion |

*Voidborn net is positive as Galaxy Scoop is superior to the 4 blocked ramscoops

### Playstyle Comparison

**Early Game:**
- **Fastest Expansion:** Voidborn (fuel efficiency + IT bonuses)
- **Strongest Economy:** Crystallids (mining + recycling)
- **Best Scouts:** Pyreans (stealth scanners)
- **Most Flexible:** Terrans (fast research)

**Mid Game:**
- **Strongest Defense:** Crystallids (jammers, dual shields)
- **Best Intelligence:** Pyreans (cloaked scouts, cargo theft)
- **Territorial Control:** Voidborn (range + terraforming)
- **Tech Leader:** Terrans (balanced research progression)

**Late Game:**
- **Most Powerful:** Pyreans (stealth fleet with advanced tech)
- **Most Resilient:** Crystallids (defensive fleet, resource recovery)
- **Largest Empire:** Voidborn (expansion advantages)
- **Most Advanced:** Terrans (tech level advantage)

### Strategic Matchups

**Pyreans vs Crystallids:**
- Pyreans have stealth advantage but Crystallids counter with Cloak Detection
- Crystallids can't effectively bomb Pyrean planets (no smart bombs)
- Advantage: Pyreans (stealth > detection)

**Voidborn vs Terrans:**
- Voidborn expand faster but Terrans have tech superiority
- Voidborn mineral alchemy counters resource pressure
- Advantage: Even (mobility vs technology)

**Crystallids vs Voidborn:**
- Crystallids have superior defense but limited offense
- Voidborn have range to strike and retreat
- Advantage: Voidborn (mobility vs static defense)

**Terrans vs Pyreans:**
- Terrans have no stealth counter (no Cloak Detection)
- Pyreans slow expansion vulnerable to Terran pressure
- Advantage: Terrans (can rush before Pyrean tech dominates)

## Technical Implementation

The racial trait system is implemented through a data-driven filtering pipeline that prevents players from seeing or using components they don't have access to.

### Core System Components

**1. Type Definitions** (`src/app/data/tech-atlas.types.ts`):
```typescript
export type PrimaryRacialTrait =
  | 'Hyper Expansion'
  | 'Super Stealth'
  | 'War Monger'
  // ... 10 total

export type LesserRacialTrait =
  | 'Improved Fuel Efficiency'
  | 'Total Terraforming'
  // ... 14 total

export interface ComponentStats {
  // ... other properties
  primaryRacialTraitRequired?: PrimaryRacialTrait[];
  primaryRacialTraitUnavailable?: PrimaryRacialTrait[];
  lesserRacialTraitRequired?: LesserRacialTrait[];
  lesserRacialTraitUnavailable?: LesserRacialTrait[];
}
```

**2. Species Model** (`src/app/models/game.model.ts`):
```typescript
export interface Species {
  id: string;
  name: string;
  habitat: { /* ... */ };
  traits: Array</* economic modifiers */>;
  primaryTraits?: PrimaryRacialTrait[];
  lesserTraits?: LesserRacialTrait[];
}
```

**3. Trait Filtering Logic** (`src/app/services/tech.service.ts` lines 129-198):
```typescript
isComponentAllowedByTraits(component: ComponentStats, player: Player): boolean {
  // 1. Check Primary Racial Trait Required (player must have ALL)
  if (component.primaryRacialTraitRequired?.length > 0) {
    const hasAllRequired = component.primaryRacialTraitRequired.every(req =>
      player.species.primaryTraits?.includes(req)
    );
    if (!hasAllRequired) return false;
  }

  // 2. Check Lesser Racial Trait Unavailable (player cannot have ANY)
  if (component.lesserRacialTraitUnavailable?.length > 0) {
    const hasAnyForbidden = component.lesserRacialTraitUnavailable.some(forbidden =>
      player.species.lesserTraits?.includes(forbidden)
    );
    if (hasAnyForbidden) return false;
  }

  // 3. Check Primary Racial Trait Unavailable (player cannot have ANY)
  if (component.primaryRacialTraitUnavailable?.length > 0) {
    const hasAnyForbidden = component.primaryRacialTraitUnavailable.some(forbidden =>
      player.species.primaryTraits?.includes(forbidden)
    );
    if (hasAnyForbidden) return false;
  }

  // 4. Check Lesser Racial Trait Required (player must have ALL)
  if (component.lesserRacialTraitRequired?.length > 0) {
    const hasAllRequired = component.lesserRacialTraitRequired.every(req =>
      player.species.lesserTraits?.includes(req)
    );
    if (!hasAllRequired) return false;
  }

  return true;
}
```

### Filtering Rules

The trait checking uses the following logic:

1. **Required Traits (AND Logic):** If a component has `primaryRacialTraitRequired: ['A', 'B']`, the player must have BOTH trait A AND trait B
2. **Unavailable Traits (OR Logic):** If a component has `primaryRacialTraitUnavailable: ['A', 'B']`, the player cannot have trait A OR trait B (having either blocks the component)
3. **Evaluation Order:** Traits are checked BEFORE tech levels in the filtering pipeline
4. **Empty Arrays:** Components with no trait restrictions are available to all species (subject to tech level requirements)

## Future Enhancements

### Planned Features

1. **Species Customization:**
   - Point-buy system for creating custom species
   - Advantages cost points, disadvantages provide points
   - Validation for incompatible trait combinations

2. **UI Improvements:**
   - Trait requirement badges on component cards
   - Tooltip explaining why components are unavailable
   - Filter toggle: "Show unavailable components"
   - Trait selection screen in new game setup

3. **More Trait-Locked Components:**
   - War Monger exclusive weapons (currently has 0 components)
   - Alternate Reality unique components
   - Packet Physics mass driver weapons
   - Balance component distribution across all PRTs

4. **AI Trait Awareness:**
   - AI designs ships considering trait restrictions
   - AI exploits enemy trait weaknesses
   - AI values components it can actually use

5. **Trait-Based Events:**
   - Random events based on species traits
   - JOAT: Research breakthrough events
   - Inner Strength: Biological weapon immunity events
   - Super Stealth: Espionage opportunities

### Balance Adjustments

Monitor these areas for potential balance issues:

1. **Pyreans Dominance:** 14 exclusive components may be overpowered. Consider:
   - Increasing Bleeding Edge Technology cost penalty
   - Reducing stealth effectiveness percentages
   - Adding more counter-stealth options for other species

2. **Crystallids Weakness:** Net -2 components and no smart bombs. Consider:
   - Buffing mining bonuses (ARM, Ultimate Recycling)
   - Adding more defensive exclusive components
   - Reducing smart bomb effectiveness to make loss less impactful

3. **Unused PRTs:** War Monger, Alternate Reality, Packet Physics have no components. Plan:
   - Design unique components for each
   - Ensure each PRT has 3-5 exclusive components minimum
   - Balance new components carefully
